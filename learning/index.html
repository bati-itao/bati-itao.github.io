Teleworking Essentials

During a time of crisis (e.g., the COVID-19 pandemic), the Government of Canada (GOC ) network is limited to essential employees, with few exceptions. This page contains information pertaining to working offline from the GOC network that includes: tools and applications for continuation of work, team communication software and a listing of tasks that can/should be completed.

Software

The IT Strategy page has a listing of tools that can be leveraged for various aspects of work such as: instant text chats and live document editing.

To Do

Some personal employee objects may still be completed while teleworking (offline from the GOD network), such as:

  • Mandatory saba courses
  • Studying or training for courses and certifications

Bonus

Please visit our Accessibility Curriculum section for ideas on free accessibility courses you can participate in while working at home.

Saba

Saba is available on the world wide web at esdc.sabacloud, this means you can access it while offline from the GOC network.

Note: If you are having trouble logging in, try refreshing (press f5 or click the refresh icon in your browser window: ⟳) the page and input your login information again. If there is still a problem, reset your password or email your issue to the Saba team.

Mandatory training

On your Saba dashboard, there should be a mandatory training item under the my learning section. Activate (click) the view details (under mandatory training) button and a new page with all the mandatory courses should appear. If here is no mandatory training item on your dashboard or your list does not contain all the courses, below is a listing of the courses and their codes.
