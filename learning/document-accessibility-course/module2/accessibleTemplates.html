Why templates help

  • Provide consistent heading hierarchy and reading order.
  • Reduce manual formatting and errors.
  • Streamline navigation for assistive technologies.

Do this

  • Use built-in Heading 1–3 styles for structure.
  • Use true lists and tables (no tabbed text or drawn lines).
  • Set the Title property and document metadata.
  • Ensure slide masters define reading order left-to-right, top-to-bottom.

Avoid

  • Text boxes for body content (they often create reading order problems).
  • Images of text (use real text; reserve images for essential graphics).
  • Color-only cues for emphasis or required fields.

Check your work

  • Use the Accessibility Checker in your authoring tool.
  • Tab through content to check focus order where applicable.
  • Export to PDF and verify the tag structure if you publish PDFs.
