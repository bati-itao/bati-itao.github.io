Alternative text basics

Alternative text (alt text) describes images for people who cannot see them, including users of screen readers. Good alt text conveys the same information or function that the image provides.

In emails, images may not load properly due to security settings, making alt text even more important for all users.

Adding alt text in Outlook

Follow these steps to add alt text to images in Outlook:

  1. Right-click on the image
  2. Select "Edit Alt Text" from the context menu
  3. Type a brief description of the image
  4. Click OK to save

Tip: You can also select the image and use the Alt Text button in the Picture Format tab.

Writing effective alt text

Good alt text is concise, descriptive, and provides the essential information the image conveys.

Guidelines for writing alt text:

  • Be concise: Aim for 125 characters or less
  • Be specific: Describe what's important about the image
  • Skip obvious words: Don't start with "Image of" or "Picture of"
  • Include context: Consider why the image is in the email
  • Describe function: For functional images, describe what happens when clicked

Decorative images

Some images are purely decorative and don't convey important information. For these images, mark them as decorative so screen readers skip them.

To mark an image as decorative:

  1. Right-click on the image
  2. Select "Edit Alt Text"
  3. Check the "Mark as decorative" box
  4. Click OK

Examples

   Good examples

Chart or graph:

Alt text: "Bar chart showing 25% increase in sales from Q1 to Q2"

Company logo:

Alt text: "ABC Company logo"

Product photo:

Alt text: "Blue wireless headphones with adjustable headband"

Decorative image:

Action: Mark as decorative (no alt text needed)

Good practice ends

   Bad examples

Too vague:

Bad: "Image" or "Picture"

Better: Describe what the image actually shows

Too wordy:

Bad: "This is a photograph that shows our team of five people standing in the office conference room during our weekly meeting on Tuesday morning"

Better: "Team photo: five people in conference room"

Bad practice ends

Best practices

  • Add alt text to all meaningful images
  • Mark decorative images as decorative
  • Keep alt text concise but descriptive
  • Consider the context and purpose of the image
  • Test your emails with images disabled to see how alt text works
  • Don't include "image of" or "picture of" in alt text
  • For complex images, consider providing additional description in the email text
