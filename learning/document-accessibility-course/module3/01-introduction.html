Overview

PowerPoint accessibility ensures that presentations can be understood and used by all people, including those with disabilities. Creating accessible presentations from the start is more efficient than retrofitting them later and ensures inclusive communication for all audiences.

Why PowerPoint accessibility matters

Accessible presentations:

  • Ensure all team members and stakeholders can participate fully in meetings and presentations
  • Comply with accessibility legislation and organizational policies
  • Improve usability for everyone, not just people with disabilities
  • Support various assistive technologies like screen readers
  • Enable proper conversion to other accessible formats

Key accessibility principles

The fundamental principles of accessible PowerPoint design include:

Perceivable
Information must be presentable in ways users can perceive through sight, sound, or touch
Operable
Interface components must be operable through various input methods
Understandable
Information and operation of the interface must be understandable
Robust
Content must work with various assistive technologies
