Microsoft Teams accessible services for users

This guide is an overview of MS Teams accessibility features and is intended for EDSC employees. Some of the accessibility best practices within this platform are described.

Host/Presenter features

Record a meeting

Source: Take advantage of the accessibility features in MS Teams for a better meeting or live event experience

When possible and with the consent of all participants, set the meeting to be recorded. A meeting recording can be helpful for people with cognitive difficulties, for example: you can go through the recording at your own pace as well as go back to double-check anything you might have missed.

Notes:

  • Before you start recording, make sure the other participants approve.
  • Ensure that all video recordings have accurate, time synced closed captions.

To start and stop recording in a meeting, go to your meeting controls and select or tab to Start recording or Stop recording. You can find the recording in the meeting chat history. To open the chat history, go to your meeting controls and select Show conversation. If you use Microsoft Outlook, a link to the meeting recording will also be sent to your email.

Spotlight a video

Source: Spotlight someone's video in a MS Teams meeting (microsoft.com)

Spotlighting a video is putting the video in focus for all participants in the meeting. If you're an organizer or a presenter, up to seven people's video feeds (including your own) can be highlighted.

  • Right-click on the person's video and select Spotlight from the menu (or Add spotlight, if someone else is already spotlighted).
  • Or, select or tab to the Show participants menu button.
  • Tab to In this meeting to be provided with a list of everyone in the meeting. Find the name of the person in the list whose video you want to highlight, right-click on their name, and select Spotlight, or when focus is on the their name press Shift + F10 and select Spotlight.
  • When you're ready to end the spotlight, right-click again and choose Stop spotlighting If multiple people are spotlighted, you can stop spotlighting everyone at once under Show participants, or tabtoShow participants menu button, tab to the In this meetingsection, and find the name of the person being spotlighted, press Shift + F10 and unspotlight the video.

This is helpful if there are multiple videos, or people joining the meeting late. It ensures that the speaker’s video will remain in focus as the main part of the screen.

Share PowerPoint presentation

Source: Share PowerPoint slides in a MS Teams meeting (microsoft.com)

By sharing the PowerPoint directly in MS Teams, both the presenter and the meeting attendees can view and move through the slide deck. The presenter can easily share a slide from anywhere in the deck, and attendees can absorb the content at their own pace; this also make it accessible for users who rely on screen readers.

Begin sharing a PowerPoint deck

  1. Select Share content in your meeting controls or press Ctrl+shift+E to open Share content
  2. Press tab to navigate to the PowerPoint section listed options and select the file you want from the list of available files, or tab to Browse OneDrive, or tab to Browse my computer to find a file that is not listed.
  3. After selecting your file by default, the presenter view is selected: displays your presentation notes to the right of the slide you’re currently presenting, and a thumbnail strip of upcoming slides along the bottom.
  4. Focus will default to be on the first slide in the PowerPoint deck. Using the directional arrow keys will allow you to move through the slides.
  5. To remove the presentation notes and the thumbnail strip of slides, select or tab to More actions from below the current slide then select Hide presenter view. Alternativly press Ctrl+Shift+x and the presentation notes and thumbnail strip will be removed from the screen. Pressing Ctrl+Shift+x a second time will display the presentation notes and thumbnail strip on your screen again.

Notes

  • If you select one of these files to present, all meeting participants will be able to view the slides during the meeting. Their access permissions to the file outside of the meeting won't change.
  • If you select Browse and choose to present a PowerPoint file that hasn't been uploaded to MS Teams before, it will get uploaded as part of the meeting.
  • If you're presenting in a channel meeting, the file is uploaded to the Files tab in the channel, where all team members will have access to it.

Start live transcription

Source: View live transcription in a MS Teams meeting (microsoft.com)

Note: as of October 2021 the live transcription option in MS Teams is currently being tested for departmental release and is not yet currently available for all employees.During a scheduled meeting, you can start a live transcription. The text appears alongside the meeting video or audio in real time, including the speaker's name (unless they chose to hide it) and a time stamp.Live transcription is helpful for people who are deaf or hard-of-hearing, in a noisy environment, or have different levels of language proficiency.

  • Go to the meeting controls and select or tab to More actions
  • Choose Start transcription.

To change the language:

  • Go to the Transcript panel and select the More button.
  • Select the Change spoken language option.
  • Choose the arrow down button to select the spoken language of the meeting.
  • Confirm choice

Set the noise suppression level to high

Source: Reduce background noise in MS Teams meetings (microsoft.com)

When your mic is turned on in a MS Teams meeting or call, background noise can distract others. MS Teams offers three levels of noise suppression for the MS Teams app to help keep meeting participants focused. Once changed, the setting carries over to your next meeting or call.

  • Select or tab to More actionsin your meeting controls and then selectDevice settings
  • Under Noise suppression, select an option. (See step 2 above for option descriptions.)

Add someone to a call

Source: Add someone to a call in MS Teams (microsoft.com)

When you're already on a call, you might need to add someone such as an interpreter to a call.

To add someone new to a group call, select or tab to Show participants , then type their name or phone number in the search box to find the persons name and then select send invite.

Change your background during a meeting

Source: Take advantage of the accessibility features in MS Teams for a better meeting or live event experience

Reducing visual distractions can be especially helpful for persons with visual impairments or cognitive difficulties. When the background is blurred or there is a steady image, it is easier to focus on the person speaking.

  • To alter your background while in a meeting, go to your meeting controls and select or tab to More actions > Show background effects.
  • Select Blur to blur your background, or choose from the available images to replace it. To upload an image of your own, select Add new and pick an image from your computer.
  • You can preview your chosen background to see how it looks before you apply it.

Notes

  • If your meeting uses a sign language interpreter, don't blur their background, as this can obscure sign language.

Attendee features

Join a meeting

Source: Join a meeting in MS Teams (microsoft.com)

Join by link

Select Click here to join the meeting in your meeting invite to be taken to a page where you can choose to either join on the web or download the desktop app.

Join from calendar

Select Calendar on the left side of MS Teams to view your meetings or select Ctrl+4 to open Calendar.

Find the meeting you want and select Join.

Or, if someone starts the meeting, you'll get a notification you can use to join.

Join in a channel

If a meeting takes place in a channel, you’ll see an invitation to join, relevant content, and who’s in the meeting right in the channel. Just select or tab to the Join button.

Join from chat

If the meeting has already begun, it appears in your recent chat list. Select the meeting in your chat list and then select or tab to Join button at the top of the chat.

Call in

If you're unable to use the MS Teams app or MS Teams on the web, you can join some meetings by calling a phone number. If there's a phone number and conference ID in the meeting invite, just dial the number to join. The number in your invitation may not be local to you. Select Find a local number to get one that is.

Pin a meeting participant's video

Source: Adjust your view in a MS Teams meeting (microsoft.com)

To focus on a particular video, right click and select Pin. The video will be pinned to your view regardless of who's talking. You can pin as many videos as will fit on your screen. To remove focus of the video, right click again and hit Unpin.

MS Teams does not allow pinned videos to be resized. A feature request has been sent to Microsoft.

Likewise to pin a video tab to the Show participants menu button and hit enter. Tab to the In this meeting section to a list of participants names in the meeting and find their name. Select Shift + F10 and select Pin.

To unpin a video, tab to Show participants menu button and hit enter. Tab to the in this meeting section to the list of participants names in the meeting and find their name. Select Shift + F10 and select Unpin.

Currently, MS Teams is unable to have multiple videos on dual screens. The Sign Language Interpretation (SLI) workaround for MS Teams is to ensure the required attendees have two separate devices to connect to the meeting, one to view the presentation, and one dedicated view for the SLI.

Turn live captions on and off

Source: Use live captions in a MS Teams meeting (microsoft.com)

Captions are available in English and French. For some early users, if French is not currently available to you it will be in upcoming updates.

  • To use live captions in a meeting, go to your meeting controls.
  • Select or tab to More actions > Turn on live captions.
  • To stop using live captions, go to the meeting controls and select or tab to More actions > Turn off live captions.

To change the language:

  • To the right of the captions, select or tab to … caption settings within the video and press enter.
  • Select Change spoken language and hit enter.
  • Choose from the drop down menu by using the arrow keys and select the spoken language of the meeting and hit enter.
  • Select or tab to Confirm choice and hit enter.

Keyboard shortcuts for MS Teams

Source: Keyboard shortcuts for MS Teams

Using keyboard shortcuts can help users work more efficiently. For users with mobility or vision disabilities, keyboard shortcuts are an essential alternative to using a mouse.

Notes

  • The shortcuts refer to the US keyboard layout. Keys for other layouts might not correspond exactly to the keys on a US keyboard.
  • If a shortcut requires pressing two or more keys at the same time, this topic separates the keys with a plus sign (+). If you have to press one key immediately after another, the keys are separated by a comma (,).
  • You can easily access the keyboard shortcuts list from within MS Teams. Press Ctrl+E to go to the Search field, type /keys, and then press Enter.

Show keyboard shortcuts

You can access the keyboard shortcuts directly from within the app in several ways.

  • Press Ctrl+Period (.).
  • Press Ctrl+E to go to the Search field, type /keys, and then press Enter.
  • Select the > or press Ctrl+Comma (,) to open the Settings and more button in the upper-right corner of MS Teams, and then select Keyboard shortcuts.
  • To navigate to the keyboard shortcuts with a screen reader, press the Tab key until you hear "Settings and more," and press Enter. Press the Down arrow key until you hear "Keyboard shortcuts," and press Enter.

    Change your status in MS Teams

    Source: Change your status in MS Teams (microsoft.com)

    To make sure people know when you’re busy or away from your desk, set your status in MS Teams. The little dot on your profile indicates if you’re available or not. MS Teams will automatically set the status in some cases, like when you’re in a call.

    • To set it yourself, go to your profile or tab to your profile at the top of MS Teams and hit enter, and select an option from the list. By default focus will be on your status. Hit enter again to open a list of MS Teams status and using the arrow keys find the status you want and hit enter.
    • You can also update your status from the command box. Type /available,/busy,/dnd,/brb,/away, or /offline to set your status as Available, Busy, Do not disturb, Be right back, Away, or Offline.

    Status descriptions:

    • Available: is when you’re active in MS Teams and don’t have anything in your calendar (no meetings or calls, for example). Note that MS Teams will automatically set your status from Available to Away when you lock your computer or when it enters idle or sleep mode.
    • Busy is when you want to focus on something, and you do want notifications to pop up. If you’re in a meeting or call, MS Teams will automatically change your status to In a meeting or In a call (Busy) when not set to Do not disturb.
    • Do not disturb is when you want to focus or present your screen and don’t want notificationsto pop up.
    • Be right back is when you want to say you’re temporarily away. It’s never set automatically.
    • Appear away is when you need to work without responding right away.
    • Appear offline is when you want to indicate that you're not signed in to MS Teams, so will not be responding until you're back online. You'll still receive notifications if anyone messages you.

    Use Immersive Reader in MS Teams to customize reading

    Source: Use Immersive Reader in MS Teams

    1. Hover over or tab to a message with your cursor to add focus to the message.
    2. Select or tab to More actions from the message options, and then select Immersive Reader from the provided list. Immersive Reader will launch as a full-screen experience.

    Immersive Reader features

    • Select Play to hear your document read aloud and to see the text highlighted simultaneously. The narrator starts reading from the top of your page by default, unless you have selected a word, in which case it will start reading from the selected word.
    • Select Pause to stop narration.
    • Select Voice Settings on the top right of the document to change the speed of narration.
    • Select Text Preferences and set Text Size,Spacing, and Themes that suit your needs.
    • Switch on Show Source Formatting to retain formatting from the original text, such as bolding or underlining.
    • Select Close to exit Immersive Reader.

    Search for messages and more in MS Teams

    Source: Search for messages and more in MS Teams (microsoft.com)

    There are search options to help find messages, people, files, and other information shared in MS Teams.

    • Start any search from the command box at the top of the app.
    • Press Ctrl+E to go to the command box.
    • Type a word or name, and MS Teams will start to make suggestions.
    • Either select one of the suggested items, or press Enter to get a full list of results.
    • A search page with a list will populate, press tab to move through the list to find the message you want.
    • Select one of the items to get a full view of the item.

    Search your current location with Ctrl+F

    Use Ctrl+F to search messages within a particular chat or channel in MS Teams.

    • Select the area you want to search.
    • Type Ctrl+F and your search terms.
    • Press Enter.
    • All the search results will be displayed from within that chat or channel conversation.
    • Select any one of the available results and MS Teams will navigate to the result.

    Search for one-on-one or group chats

    • Type the person’s name in the command box at the top of the app.
    • Their name and any group chats listed will appear.
    • Select their name to go to your one-on-one chat with them, or select a group chat.
    • Or, search for group chats by the chat name (if it has one) or search by the name of the chat participants.

    Search for teams and channels

    • Search for a team or channel by its name in the command box.
    • Click on the suggested option in the list to directly navigate to the Team or channel.

    Search for messages

    • Start typing a keyword into the command box at the top of the app.
    • Press Enter.
    • A list of results that include the keyword will appear.

    Search tips

    • Type the first few letters of a word followed immediately by an asterisk *.
    • Example: type "Serv*" into the command box at the top of the app, and you'll get results for all words that start with those four letters (e.g. server, service, etc).
    • Use quotation marks to search for an exact match on a word or phrase.
    • Example: “annual budget”.
